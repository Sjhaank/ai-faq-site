1. What is Artificial Intelligence and when was it founded?

The Birth and Evolution of Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence in machines programmed to think, learn, and problem-solve like humans.

1950: Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the Turing Test
1956: The term "Artificial Intelligence" coined at Dartmouth Conference by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Nathaniel Rochester, and Claude Shannon
1997: IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov
2016: Google's AlphaGo defeats Go world champion Lee Sedol
2022: ChatGPT launched, making AI accessible to mainstream users
üèÜ Key Founding Figures:
  • John McCarthy (1927-2011): Coined the term "AI" and developed LISP programming language
  • Marvin Minsky (1927-2016): Co-founder of MIT's AI Laboratory
  • Alan Turing (1912-1954): Theoretical foundation with the Turing Test
  • Claude Shannon (1916-2001): Father of Information Theory
üí° Fun Fact: The 1956 Dartmouth Conference lasted 6 weeks and included only 10 participants, but it laid the foundation for the entire field of AI research!

2. What is Machine Learning and how does it work?

Machine Learning - Teaching Machines to Learn

Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that enables computers to learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed for every scenario.

1959: Arthur Samuel coins the term "Machine Learning"
1986: Backpropagation algorithm popularized by Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams
2006: Geoffrey Hinton introduces deep learning concept
2012: AlexNet wins ImageNet competition, deep learning revolution begins

Types of Machine Learning:

  • Supervised Learning: Learning with labeled examples (classification, regression)
  • Unsupervised Learning: Finding patterns in unlabeled data (clustering)
  • Reinforcement Learning: Learning through rewards and penalties (game playing, robotics)
üéØ ML Pioneers:
  • Geoffrey Hinton: "Godfather of Deep Learning," developed backpropagation
  • Yann LeCun: Convolutional Neural Networks, Facebook AI Research
  • Yoshua Bengio: Deep learning research, University of Montreal
  • Andrew Ng: Stanford AI Lab, Coursera, Google Brain, Baidu

3. What is Generative AI and how has it revolutionized artificial intelligence?

Generative AI - The Creative Revolution

Generative AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that can create new content - text, images, code, music, videos - that resembles human-created content.

2014: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) introduced by Ian Goodfellow
2017: Transformer architecture introduced in "Attention Is All You Need" paper
2020: GPT-3 released with 175B parameters, breakthrough in language generation
2022: ChatGPT launched (November 30), reaches 100M users in 2 months
2023: GPT-4 released, Google Bard launched, Claude 2 released
2024: Claude 3, Gemini Ultra, Sora (video generation) released

Types of Generative AI Models:

  • Large Language Models: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, LLaMA
  • Text-to-Image: DALL-E 3, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion
  • Text-to-Video: Sora, RunwayML, Pika Labs
  • Code Generation: GitHub Copilot, CodeT5, Amazon CodeWhisperer
  • Audio/Music: MusicLM, AIVA, Jukebox
üåü Generative AI Pioneers:
  • Ian Goodfellow: Invented GANs at University of Montreal (2014)
  • Vaswani et al.: Google researchers who created Transformer architecture
  • Alec Radford: Led GPT development at OpenAI
  • Aditya Ramesh: Created DALL-E at OpenAI

4. What is Google's Agent Development Kit (ADK) and how does it work?

Google Agent ADK - Building Intelligent AI Agents

Google's Agent Development Kit (ADK) is a comprehensive framework for building intelligent AI agents that can interact with users, access external tools, and perform complex tasks.

2021: Google announces LaMDA (Language Model for Dialogue Applications)
2022: Vertex AI Search and Conversation launched
2023: Bard chatbot launched, Google releases PaLM 2
2023 (Dec): Gemini models announced (Nano, Pro, Ultra)
2024: Agent Development Kit (ADK) released for building custom agents
2024: Gemini 2.0 Flash released with enhanced agent capabilities

Core Components of ADK:

  • Agent Framework: Base structure for building intelligent agents
  • Tool Integration: Connect agents to external APIs and services
  • Conversation Management: Handle multi-turn conversations with context
  • Vertex AI Integration: Access to Google's AI models and services
  • Search Tools: Built-in search capabilities for knowledge retrieval
  • Memory Systems: Persistent and session-based memory management

ADK Agent Types:

  • Search Agents: Access and query knowledge bases, web search
  • Conversational Agents: Handle natural language interactions
  • Tool-Using Agents: Integrate with external APIs and services
  • Multimodal Agents: Process text, images, audio, and video

6. What is Computer Vision and its applications?

Computer Vision - Teaching Machines to See

Computer Vision enables computers to interpret and understand visual information from the world, including images and videos.

1998: Yann LeCun develops LeNet for digit recognition
2012: AlexNet wins ImageNet, deep learning revolution begins
2015: ResNet introduces skip connections
2020: CLIP by OpenAI connects vision and language

Applications:

  • Image Classification: Identifying objects in images
  • Facial Recognition: Security systems, photo tagging
  • Medical Imaging: X-ray analysis, cancer detection
  • Autonomous Vehicles: Road sign recognition, pedestrian detection
  • Quality Control: Manufacturing defect detection
üéØ Computer Vision Pioneers:

7. What is Natural Language Processing (NLP)?

Natural Language Processing - Understanding Human Language

NLP helps computers understand, interpret, and generate human language in a meaningful way.

1966: ELIZA chatbot created by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT
2013: Word2Vec introduces word embeddings (Tomas Mikolov, Google)
2017: Transformer architecture revolutionizes NLP
2018: BERT introduces bidirectional understanding

Core NLP Tasks:

  • Sentiment Analysis: Determining emotional tone of text
  • Machine Translation: Converting text between languages
  • Question Answering: Providing answers to natural language questions
  • Text Summarization: Creating concise summaries
  • Named Entity Recognition: Identifying people, places, organizations
üß† NLP Pioneers:

  • Tomas Mikolov: Word2Vec creator, Facebook AI Research

  • Jacob Devlin: BERT creator at Google

8. What is Reinforcement Learning and its applications?

Reinforcement Learning - Learning Through Trial and Error

Reinforcement Learning (RL) is where agents learn to make decisions by interacting with an environment, receiving rewards or penalties for their actions.

1989: Q-Learning algorithm developed by Chris Watkins
2013: Deep Q-Network (DQN) by DeepMind - deep RL breakthrough
2016: AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol in Go (DeepMind)
2017: AlphaZero masters chess, shogi, and Go
2022: ChatGPT uses RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)

Applications:

  • Game Playing: Chess, Go, Poker, video games
  • Robotics: Robot navigation, manipulation
  • Autonomous Vehicles: Path planning, decision making
  • Finance: Algorithmic trading, portfolio optimization
  • Language Models: RLHF for ChatGPT alignment
üéÆ RL Pioneers:
  • Richard Sutton: "Father of RL," University of Alberta
  • Chris Watkins: Invented Q-Learning algorithm
  • Demis Hassabis: DeepMind founder, AlphaGo creator
  • David Silver: Led AlphaGo and AlphaZero projects
üèÜ Achievement: RLHF is the key technology that made ChatGPT helpful, harmless, and honest!

9. What are AI Agents and how do they work?
